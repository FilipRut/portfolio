Reimagining the digital experience for 15M+ users

The Challenge

Orange Poland's customer portal was built on legacy patterns that no longer served modern users. Navigation was complex, task completion rates were below 40%, and the visual language was inconsistent across 12+ product touchpoints. Support tickets were rising 15% quarter over quarter.

Who We Designed For

Primary users fell into two segments: **self-service power users** (35-55, managing family plans, troubleshooting independently) and **first-time digital users** (55+, recently migrated from in-store to online). Both groups struggled with the same core flows — billing, plan changes, and device management — but for different reasons.

Discovery & Insights

Led a 6-week discovery phase combining quantitative analytics with qualitative research:
- **42 user interviews** across both segments
- **Journey mapping** of 8 critical user flows
- **Competitive audit** of 6 telecom portals (T-Mobile, Play, Plus, Vodafone, O2, EE)
- **Heatmap analysis** revealing 68% of users never scrolled past the first fold

Three key insights emerged: users couldn't find what they needed (IA problem), they didn't trust the actions they took (feedback problem), and they abandoned flows that required more than 3 steps (complexity problem).

Approach

Defined three design principles that guided every decision:
1. **Progressive disclosure** — show only what's needed, reveal complexity on demand
2. **Confidence at every step** — real-time validation, clear confirmation states
3. **One flow, one goal** — no multi-purpose screens, each view serves a single task

Built a modular design system (120+ components) that enforced these principles at the component level — making it harder to build bad UX than good UX.

The Redesign

Rebuilt the portal's information architecture from scratch, reducing top-level navigation from 12 items to 5. Introduced a task-oriented dashboard replacing the feature-oriented homepage. Every critical flow was redesigned to complete in 3 steps or fewer, with inline validation and contextual help.

Impact

Measured over 3 months post-launch against the same period prior year.

+34% Task completion rate
-45% Support ticket volume
2x Faster onboarding for new features
4.2★ App Store rating (from 2.8★)

What I Learned

The biggest lesson was around stakeholder alignment. We spent the first 2 months in discovery, which felt slow — but having data-backed design decisions meant zero pushback during implementation. If I could do it again, I'd involve engineering earlier in the design system phase to catch token naming conflicts before they became tech debt.

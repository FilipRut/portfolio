problem:
  heading: "The Challenge"
  body: "Zabka's existing app had 47 features but users only engaged with 3. The ordering flow took 8 taps from launch to confirmation. In a brand built on speed and convenience, the digital experience was the slowest part of the customer journey."

users:
  heading: "Target Users"
  body: "Urban commuters (22-40) who visit Zabka 3-5x per week. They know exactly what they want. Every second of friction in the app is a reason to just walk in and grab it manually instead."

research:
  heading: "What We Found"
  body: |
    - **Shadowed 20 users** during real store visits — average in-store time was 47 seconds
    - **App analytics** showed 72% of orders contained ≤3 items
    - **Competitive review** of Biedronka, Starbucks, and 7-Eleven apps
    - Key insight: users didn't want to *browse* — they wanted to **reorder** their usual items instantly

strategy:
  heading: "Design Principles"
  body: |
    1. **3-tap max** — from app open to order confirmed
    2. **Memory over discovery** — surface recent/frequent orders, not categories
    3. **Reward speed** — gamified streaks for consecutive days of ordering

solution:
  heading: "The Concept"
  body: "Replaced the traditional category-based home screen with a smart reorder feed. Your last 3 orders are one tap away. New 'Streak' system rewards daily ordering with progressive discounts. In-store pickup uses NFC — walk in, tap phone, grab bag, leave."

results:
  heading: "Validation"
  body: "Tested with 30 users in Maze prototype sessions."
  metrics:
    - value: "3 taps"
      label: "Launch to order confirmed"
    - value: "92%"
      label: "Task success rate (prototype)"
    - value: "4.6/5"
      label: "Ease of use score"

reflection:
  heading: "Takeaways"
  body: "Concept was approved for development backlog. The biggest challenge was convincing stakeholders to remove features rather than add them. The 47→3 feature reduction felt radical but the data supported it. Working with Protopie for micro-interactions helped sell the 'premium speed' feeling that static mockups couldn't convey."
